## Surface

New file: `agentable-canvas/src/canvas/panelImports.ts`
- Single source of truth for the 7 lazy panel `import()` functions
- `prefetchPanel(key)` — fire-and-forget single-panel warm
- `prefetchAllPanelsIdle()` — warms every panel after first paint via `requestIdleCallback` (1.5s `setTimeout` fallback for Safari < 17 / older WebViews); returns cancel fn

Modified:
- `src/canvas/CanvasShell.tsx` — `lazy()` calls now consume from `panelImports.*`. `useEffect` calls `prefetchAllPanelsIdle()` once per mount, returns its cancel fn for unmount cleanup.
- `src/canvas/NavSidebar.tsx` — added `prefetchKey: PanelImportKey` to each nav item that maps to a lazy chunk; `onPointerEnter` and `onFocus` (collapsed view) trigger `prefetchPanel(key)` for the ~100ms head-start before click. `chat` and `resume` items have no `prefetchKey` because they open eager surfaces (no chunk to warm).

## Why

Two-tier prefetch covers both warm and cold sessions:
- **Idle** — most users sit on the canvas a few seconds before clicking anything; warming all 7 chunks during that window means first click is instant from the module cache.
- **Hover** — for fast clickers / cold-load sessions where idle hasn't fired yet, `onPointerEnter` gives ~100ms of headroom on a 100–400ms chunk fetch. Pure win on cold; no-op on warm (same `import()` deduped by Vite).

Vite chunk-dedupes — calling the same `import()` from `lazy()` and from prefetch hits one chunk, fetched at most once.

## Trade-offs / known limits

- The cast `as Parameters<typeof lazy>[0]` in `CanvasShell.tsx` works around React's `lazy<T extends ComponentType<any>>` constraint — `panelImports.*` is typed as `() => Promise<unknown>` (uniform shape across the map). Reviewer call: cleaner with a generic `LazyComponentLoader<T>` helper, or fine as-is given each lazy() use is in one file?
- The `console.error(reason)` is intentionally absent from `prefetchPanel`'s `.catch()` — a background warm-ahead surfacing as a console error would flood dev consoles for every offline reload. Visible failures route through `ChunkErrorBoundary` when the user actually clicks the panel.
- Idle prefetch fires 1.5s after mount on browsers without `requestIdleCallback`. Open question: is 1.5s the right value? Faster (500ms) competes with the conversation's first response; slower (3s) means cold first-clicks during the gap.
